Extra portfolio pieces



U.M.M.G (Octet)

This is an arrangement of Billy strayhorn's U.M.M.G. that I did for my portfolio at RAM during my first year. Here I choose to use more modern harmony found in modern jazz as well as other styles. Halfway through the piece the metric modulation creates a change of groove that gives the piece a new dimension, even though it's mainly the same harmonic and melodic material.



Rooted and Grand (Octet)

An experimental piece that explores the harmony you get when including the #1 (sharp one) in certain chords to (in this case) create tension.

The #1 is the 7th ascending perfect fifth from the root. In this case C#, with the root note C.



Piano Sonata in A major, D. 664

This is the result of a piano sonata I arrangeded for String quartet. It was an exercise in learning more about the string instruments.



Electric Red / Electric Blue

My main piece of my 2nd year recital at the Royal Academy of Music. Here I transcribed a song by Meshuggah, and used the form and their rhytmichal patterns as the basis for originally composed melody & harmony. For quartet - Drums, Bass, Piano, and synth/keyboard.



U.M.M.G. (Lead sheet)

All my compositions for octet and big band, such as UMMG, have lead sheets that can be performed by trios and quartets.



One of Those

Composed for Jazz piano trio + Saxophone. Started of as an exploration of chords that derives from both the melodic minor, and the harmonic minor modes, as well as the #1 concept.
